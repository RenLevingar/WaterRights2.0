Outdated And Nonexistent Water Systems

Every American's right to water is protected by the state, but when the question comes to first americans this right seems to be ignored. Much of native American housing has one primary issue: there is either no water system or its outdated. Around 6% of native American housing lacks water systems meaning they need to find alternate sources of water, this can be expnsive or take up a lot of daily time. If housing does have water systems then they are likely to be outdated. This can mean that the water systems can be dangerous for human consumption meaning that they will still need to travel for their water. In comparson around 0.3% of houseing for nonindigenous people don't have water systems.

The Effects On Thier Society

In native American culture water holds great significance and sacredness. Water is known for being life-giving and healing, this means that when thier water is jepordized it affects thier cultural freedom.

Water is also an essential for human health, this means when they risk going long periods of time they are putting their health in danger. As well, many outdated water systems can prove to be dangerous due to contamination, growth of bacteria, low water quality, the decay of it's infrastructure, and inaequate long term treatment.

Native American communities are alreafy facing socioeconomic disparities, and water scarcity can further exacerbate these inequities. Limited water access can negatively remote tribes deepening existing social and economic challenges.
